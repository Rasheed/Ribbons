All you need to build an elegant responsive website.

Well, this - and the client's content - maybe a CMS of some sort - a good attitude - and a dash of javascript.

The sooner you refresh your outlook, the happier you will be. I'm not saying this is easy - I'm just saying this is a simple approach that might help you get back to the basics with a fresh view of what you really need. Dont give-in to bootstrap or other rediculous CSS "frameworks." It's much much much more complicated than just learning how basic CSS positioning works, and that is all you need - to style a webpage. Content first.

Columns:

Columns? - Eh, I'm not so sure about these.... maybe they should just be blocks?

Oh, you need some columns? Try it like this. They all start out 100% width. When you reach the desired breakpoint, you just make them the widths you want and float them accordingly. No need to call them some specific names like quarter or third in your markup. Your whole presentation layer can be in your CSS.

Target them by section if you have many different column layouts on a page. Make sure you work from small screens up.

.section04 .inner-w {float: left}
